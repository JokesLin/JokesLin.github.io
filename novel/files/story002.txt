Hermes at the Convenience Store

Chapter 1: The 3AM Visitor

At 3:17 AM, Xiao Lin was dozing against the cash register.

The automatic door chimed "ding-dong," and he reflexively looked up to say "Welcome" - then froze.

Walking in was a young man in a long dark gray trench coat, wearing a pair of very expensive-looking leather shoes, each adorned with small wing-shaped metal decorations. Most striking were his eyes, with irises the color of flowing mercury.

"Hello," the man said with a smile, his voice like melted chocolate. "Do you sell pegasus feed?"

"...What?"

"Pegasus feed. My mount Pegasus has been having some digestive issues lately. The veterinarian on Mount Olympus said he needs some mortal oats and evening primrose..."

"Sir," Xiao Lin rubbed his eyes, "this is a FamilyMart."

"I know," the man walked nonchalantly toward the shelves. "The mortal convenience store is truly a remarkable invention. Open 24 hours, complete product range, transparent pricing - the market management on Olympus compared to yours is practically the Stone Age."

He grabbed three packets of instant oatmeal, a box of evening primrose tea, two bottles of mineral water, and a bag of potato chips from the shelves.

"The potato chips are for myself," he winked. "Messengers need snacks too."

At checkout, he pulled a gold coin from his trench coat pocket.

"Sorry, we don't accept gold coins."

"How about this?" He pulled out a black card instead.

Xiao Lin swiped it - it actually went through. Total: 47.50 yuan.

"See you next time," the man picked up his shopping bag, then turned at the door. "Oh, by the way, the birthday card you sent to your mother last month - she received it at the Olympus Post Office. Very touching words."

"Wait - I haven't sent it yet!"

But the automatic door had already closed. The street outside was empty, with only the streetlights casting circles of dim yellow light.

Xiao Lin looked down at the surveillance monitor - the screen only showed himself standing at the cash register, talking to thin air.

But in the register's transaction record, there it was: 47.50 yuan.
